Format Clarity

Finland is moving to structured digital filing.

Finland was an early adopter of ESEF iXBRL in 2021. Now the Finnish Patent and Registration Office (PRH) is rolling out structured digital reporting to the broader Finnish economy through 2026–2028.

PDF-based filing

Pre-2026 · Unstructured

Finnish companies historically file annual reports as PDFs to the Trade Register. Human-readable, but not machine-parseable. Cross-border data sharing and automated validation are not possible.

Inline XBRL (iXBRL)

2026–2028 · Phased rollout

A single XHTML document with XBRL tags embedded inside. Reads as a normal financial report in a browser and is fully machine-parseable — required across ytj.fi filings and supporting EU-wide data interoperability.

Who is affected and when? Nasdaq Helsinki-listed companies have filed ESEF iXBRL since FY 2021. From 1 January 2026, all Finnish companies must submit notifications and applications electronically via ytj.fi. From 2027, companies required to appoint an auditor must submit financial statements in iXBRL. By 2028, all company accounts in Finland's Trade Register will be available in structured digital form.

Frequently Asked Questions

Finland iXBRL — your questions answered.

Is iXBRL filing already mandatory in Finland?

Partially. ESEF iXBRL has been mandatory for Nasdaq Helsinki-listed companies since financial year 2021. For the broader Finnish market, mandatory online filing via ytj.fi begins 1 January 2026, and iXBRL filing of financial statements becomes mandatory from 2027.

Who must file ESEF iXBRL with FIN-FSA / Nasdaq Helsinki?

Companies listed on Nasdaq Helsinki (issuers of shares and bonds on the regulated market) must prepare Annual Financial Reports in ESEF under the EU Transparency Directive. The requirement covers IFRS consolidated financial statements.

What is the PRH iXBRL rollout and who is affected?

The Finnish Patent and Registration Office (PRH) is rolling out mandatory structured digital filing via ytj.fi. From 2027, most Finnish companies required to appoint an auditor must submit financial statements, audit reports and management reports in iXBRL format.

What is Finland's SBR taxonomy?

SBR (Standard Business Reporting) is Finland's national taxonomy for structured digital financial reporting. It is aligned with the Finnish Accounting Standards (FAS) and supports IFRS reporting.

What is the assurance requirement on ESEF filings?

Under the Finnish Securities Markets Act amendment of 21 December 2023, an issuer's auditor must either audit the ESEF financial statements or perform an equivalent ISAE 3000 assurance engagement.

My company is listed on Helsinki First North. Do ESEF rules apply?

No. ESEF applies only to issuers on regulated markets. Companies on Nasdaq First North Growth Market Finland are outside the ESEF scope. However, you may still be affected by the PRH iXBRL mandate from 2027.
